================================================================================
VINVEST: VALUE INVESTING REPORT GENERATOR
================================================================================

Document Title: VInvest: User Requirements for Value Investment Report Generator
Version: 1.8
Date: December 1, 2025
Scope: Defines the functional and non-functional requirements for the user experience and reporting output of the VInvest application. 
Technical implementation is out of scope.

================================================================================
1. GOAL AND PERSONA
================================================================================

The VInvest application aims to empower the modern value investor by synthesizing complex financial and qualitative 
data into highly structured, clear, and actionable reports, mirroring the quality of analysis found in top-tier consulting firm insights.

Target User: The informed individual investor, financial advisor, or analyst seeking a structured, data-driven investment thesis on publicly traded companies.

================================================================================
2. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS (FR)
================================================================================

These requirements describe the specific capabilities and content the VInvest application must deliver to the user.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FR-1: COMPANY AND REPORT SELECTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ID: FR-1.1
Description: The user shall be able to search for and select a target company using its ticker symbol or full company name.

ID: FR-1.2
Description: The system shall clearly present the available report types (
        1- "Company Performance & Investment Thesis," 
        2- "Industry Deep Dive," 
        3-  "Custom Analysis").

ID: FR-1.3
Description: The user shall be able to set the time horizon for historical data included in the report (e.g., "Last 5 Years," "Last 10 Years," "Since IPO").

ID: FR-1.4
Report Refinement via Chat: The user shall be able to refine any generated report (FR-2.1, FR-2.2, FR-2.3) using the natural language chat interface.
Examples of refinements include:
- Change the length and focus: "Make this a one-page summary."
- Adjust the analytical emphasis: "Focus the conclusion on long-term debt trends."
- Target a specific audience: "Rewrite the conclusion for an institutional client."
- Add specific data points: "Include the dividend payout ratio for the last three years."




--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FR-2: INVESTMENT REPORT GENERATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The system shall generate structured reports across multiple core types of investment analysis.

================================================================================
FR-2.1: THE "COMPANY PERFORMANCE & INVESTMENT THESIS" REPORT (PRIMARY REPORT)
================================================================================

This report is designed to address the central question: "Should I buy this company, and why?" (Conclusion First Structure)

# Executive Summary & Recommendation
A concise, single-page summary presenting the final investment recommendation (e.g., Strong Buy, Hold, Sell), the rationale, and the company's competitive ranking.

# Detailed Investment Thesis Statement
Synthesis of the findings into a clear, concluding summary statement justifying the "Buy/Hold/Sell" recommendation and outlining the required conditions for the investment's success.

# Economic Moat Assessment
The report shall contain a dedicated section defining the company's sustainable competitive advantage ("Economic Moat"), categorized (e.g., Wide, Narrow, None) and supported by qualitative evidence (e.g., network effects, brand value, cost advantages).

# Industry Disruption Analysis
The report shall provide an overview of the target company's industry, including overall growth trajectory, key trends, and a specific analysis of any major impending or recent disruptions (technology, regulation, etc.) that could impact the company's moat or growth.

# Competitive Context & Peer Identification
The report shall automatically identify and list 5-10 direct, relevant industry peers.

# Relative Performance Benchmarking
The report shall compare the target company against its identified peers using the following metrics, presented in clear tables and charts: Intrinsic Value vs. Market Value Differential, Return on Invested Capital (ROIC), 5-Year Revenue Growth (CAGR), and Earnings Yield.

# Industry Ranking Score
The report shall calculate and display a clear, quantifiable ranking (e.g., "2nd of 8 Peers") based on the composite strength of the company's core value metrics (FR-2.1.6).

# Qualitative & Growth Catalysts
The report shall synthesize and highlight several key, interesting facts and the latest news that directly impact the company's future growth prospects (last 12 months).

================================================================================
FR-2.2: THE "INDUSTRY DEEP DIVE" REPORT
================================================================================

This report is designed to provide a comprehensive, top-down analysis of a selected industry, its structure, and its profitability dynamics. (Conclusion First Structure)

ID: FR-2.2.1
Report Section/Requirement: Synthesis & Strategic Recommendations
The initial section shall synthesize all core insights (Market Size, Competitive Forces, Profit Pools, Trends) into a clear, concluding thesis on the industry's overall attractiveness, highlighting 2-3 actionable investment recommendations.

ID: FR-2.2.2
Report Section/Requirement: Major Trends & Value Impact Analysis
A dedicated section analyzing the Top 3 major trends and disruptions (technological, regulatory, or economic) currently affecting the industry. It must include actionable insights on what companies should watch for and a forecast of how these trends will impact the intrinsic value and future potential of companies within the sector.

ID: FR-2.2.3
Report Section/Requirement: Industry Segmentation & Size
The report shall quantify the total addressable market (TAM) for the industry, define its key sub-segments (e.g., geographical, product type), and provide 5-year historical and projected growth rates for the overall market.

ID: FR-2.2.4
Report Section/Requirement: Competitive Forces Analysis
The report shall perform a qualitative and quantitative analysis of the industry structure based on Porter's Five Forces (e.g., Rivalry, New Entrants, Substitutes, Supplier/Buyer Power).

ID: FR-2.2.5
Report Section/Requirement: Profit Pool Mapping
The report shall visually map the industry's value chain, clearly identifying where profit pools are concentrated and predicting potential shifts in value capture over the next 3 years.

ID: FR-2.2.6
Report Section/Requirement: Key Drivers and Barriers
The report shall analyze and list the primary forces driving growth (e.g., demographic shifts, technological adoption) and the major structural barriers to entry and expansion (e.g., regulatory hurdles, capital intensity).

ID: FR-2.2.7
Report Section/Requirement: Aggregate Financial Benchmarks
The report shall present consolidated industry-wide financial metrics, including average ROIC, median debt-to-equity ratio, and average gross profit margin, benchmarked against broader sector data.

================================================================================
FR-2.3: THE "CUSTOM ANALYSIS" REPORT
================================================================================

This report is designed to allow expert users to perform ad-hoc, comparative analysis defined by natural language queries.

ID: FR-2.3.1
Report Section/Requirement: Chatbot/Query Input Mechanism
The system shall provide a chat-based natural language interface where the user can define the scope and question for the custom report (e.g., "Compare Tesla and Ford on ROIC over the last 5 years").

ID: FR-2.3.2
Report Section/Requirement: Multi-Company Comparison
The system must be able to generate tables and charts comparing a user-defined group of companies across specified value investing metrics (e.g., metrics from FR-2.1.6).

ID: FR-2.3.3
Report Section/Requirement: Multi-Industry Comparison
The system must be able to compare aggregated industry financial benchmarks (e.g., median ROIC, average revenue growth) for a user-defined set of industries.

ID: FR-2.3.4
Report Section/Requirement: Industry Performance Ranking
The system must be able to rank a user-defined set of industries based on a composite score derived from value metrics (e.g., "Which of the FANG industries is currently the most attractively valued by intrinsic value?").

ID: FR-2.3.5
Report Section/Requirement: Synthetic Narrative Output
The final report output must begin with a clear, concise conclusion that directly answers the user's query (FR-2.3.1) and provides the supporting data and visualizations underneath.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FR-3: OUTPUT AND EXPORT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The user shall be able to download the generated report in a professionally formatted PDF document, optimized for high-quality printing and sharing.
The user shall be able to view all charts and tables directly within the web application interface with interactive tooltips for data points.
The report output must include proper citation or a data source reference for all financial metrics and news snippets.
The report's design must be visually clean, professional, and minimalist, using strong white space and clear typography to prioritize readability and the flow of the narrative (McKinsey-style).

================================================================================
3. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS (NFR)
================================================================================

These requirements define the criteria used to judge the operation of the system, focusing on quality attributes.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NFR-1: DATA QUALITY AND INTEGRITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Accuracy: All quantitative metrics (e.g., ROIC, Intrinsic Value) must be calculated using consistent, verifiable methodologies and standardized financial statement data.
Timeliness: All data used in the report, including financial statements and news, must be updated and available within 24 hours of public release.
Consistency: The calculation methodology used for a metric (e.g., calculation of ROIC) shall be documented and applied consistently across all companies and reports.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NFR-2: PERFORMANCE AND SPEED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Generation Speed: The primary "Company Performance & Investment Thesis" report (FR-2.1) must be generated and rendered in the application interface within a maximum of 10 seconds of submission.
Search Latency: Company searches (FR-1.1) must return results in under 500 milliseconds.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NFR-3: APPLICATION INTERFACE AND WORKFLOW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Accessibility: The application interface must be fully responsive and optimized for use on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.
Intuition: The report generation process must be intuitive, requiring a minimal number of steps (ideally 3 clicks or less) from login to report submission.
